🚨 Common Emergencies

Plumbing Emergencies We Solve Every Day in Austin

Austin's aging infrastructure, extreme summer heat, and occasional winter freezes create the perfect conditions for plumbing disasters. Here are the most common emergencies we respond to — and why acting fast matters.

💥

Burst & Frozen Pipes

During Austin's rare but devastating winter freezes — like the February 2021 storm — thousands of pipes burst across the city. A burst pipe can release over 100 gallons per minute. Turn off your main water shut-off immediately and call us. We'll be there fast to stop the flood and repair the damage before mold sets in.

📞 Call Now
🚽

Sewer Backup & Overflow

A backed-up sewer line is both a health hazard and a major structural risk. Signs include multiple drains backing up simultaneously, gurgling sounds, and sewage odors indoors. Austin's clay-heavy soil causes aggressive root intrusion in older sewer lines — a top cause of backups in historic neighborhoods like Hyde Park and Cherrywood.

📞 Call Now
🔥

Water Heater Failure

No hot water is disruptive — but a leaking or rupturing water heater tank can cause serious water damage in minutes. If you see pooling water near your unit, hear strange popping or rumbling sounds, or notice rusty discolored water, call immediately. We stock common tank and tankless models for same-day replacement.

📞 Call Now
🏠

Slab Leaks

Austin's expansive clay soils cause homes to shift over time, stressing pipes embedded in concrete slabs. Signs include warm spots on floors, the sound of running water when all fixtures are off, or a sudden spike in your water bill. Slab leaks can silently destroy your foundation and cost tens of thousands if left untreated.

📞 Call Now
⚠️

Gas Line Leaks

If you smell rotten eggs, hear hissing sounds near gas appliances or lines, or see dead vegetation in your yard, evacuate immediately and call 911. Then call us. Our TSBPE-certified gas line technicians respond with advanced detection equipment to locate and seal leaks safely. This is never a DIY situation.

📞 Call Now
🌊

Overflowing Toilets

A toilet that won't stop running or overflowing can cause significant floor, subfloor, and structural damage within just a few hours. Whether it's a failed flapper, blocked trap, or main line stoppage, we'll diagnose and resolve it on-site — without the mess or the prolonged wait times of other Austin plumbers.

📞 Call Now
