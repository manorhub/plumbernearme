🔧 Advanced Technology

How We Locate Slab Leaks Without Tearing Up Your Floors

We invest in the industry's most precise non-destructive leak detection gear to find water line breaks beneath concrete foundations.

🔥

Thermal Imaging Cameras

Hot water slab leaks warm the surrounding concrete and flooring. We use high-resolution infrared thermal cameras to scan your floors, highlighting hot spots that indicate a hot water pipe break beneath.

🎧

Acoustic Listening Devices

Water escaping under pressure from a pipe beneath concrete makes a distinct vibration sound. We use highly sensitive ground microphones and amplifiers to listen through your slab and locate the leak to within inches.

🧲

Electromagnetic Pipe Locating

We trace the exact path of your copper or metallic pipes under the slab using electromagnetic signal locators. Mapping the pipe layout prevents unnecessary digging and helps us plan the shortest bypass route.

🎈

Nitrogen Pressure Testing

If acoustic sounds are muffled, we purge the line and charge it with safe, pressurized nitrogen gas. The gas escaping makes a louder acoustic sound that is easier to track to the exact source point.

🔄

Line Rerouting (Bypass)

Tearing up concrete is messy. Often, the best repair is running a new PEX pipe through walls or ceilings to bypass the broken underslab line entirely — leaving your concrete slab and flooring completely untouched.

🏗️

Direct Tunneling / Slab Access

If rerouting is not viable, we make precise, localized slab cuts to access and patch the leak directly. Alternatively, we tunnel under the home's foundation from the exterior, preserving your indoor flooring.

❓ FAQ

Slab Leak FAQs — Austin, TX

Common signs include a hot spot on your flooring, the sound of running water when all taps are closed, unexplained wet or warped floor patches, foundation cracks, a sudden spike in your City of Austin water bill, and mildew under carpets.
We use non-destructive detection tools including ground acoustic microphones, thermal imaging cameras, and electromagnetic pipe locators. This lets us pinpoint the leak to a precise location under the concrete, avoiding blind digging.
Electronic detection runs $150–$350. Patch repairs via direct slab cuts cost $600–$1,500. Line rerouting/bypassing (often the cleanest solution) ranges from $800–$2,200. Trenchless epoxy lining or tunneling runs higher depending on scope.
Austin's highly expansive clay soil shifts drastically during dry summers and wet winters. This movement shifts foundations and stresses underslab copper or galvanized lines. Pipe corrosion from minerals and friction wear against concrete also cause failures.
Most Texas homeowners insurance policies cover leak detection costs and resultant water damage restoration, but they may exclude the cost of the actual pipe repair. We provide insurance-compliant documentation and photos to help secure coverage.
