🔧 Our Technology

Sewer Repair Methods We Use in Austin, TX

We use the most advanced sewer repair technologies available — saving your yard, saving you money, and delivering longer-lasting results than traditional dig-and-replace methods.

📹

HD Camera Sewer Inspection

Before recommending any repair, we perform a complete HD camera inspection of your sewer line. This identifies the exact location and nature of the problem — cracks, root intrusion, bellying, offsets — so we're treating the right issue, not guessing.

🔄

Trenchless Pipe Lining (CIPP)

Cured-in-place pipe (CIPP) lining inserts a flexible epoxy-saturated liner into your existing pipe and cures it hard. Creates a new pipe within the old one — no excavation needed. Ideal for cracked or deteriorated pipes. Guaranteed to last 50+ years.

💣

Pipe Bursting

For pipes that are too damaged to line, pipe bursting simultaneously destroys the old pipe while pulling a new HDPE pipe through the same path. Trenchless, minimal disruption, and installs a pipe that's actually larger than the original for better flow.

🌿

Root Cutting & Hydro-Jetting

Austin's live oaks and cedar elms send aggressive roots into sewer lines. We use mechanical root cutters combined with high-pressure hydro-jetting to fully remove roots and restore full pipe capacity — especially critical in Hyde Park, Cherrywood, and Allandale.

🏗️

Traditional Sewer Replacement

When trenchless isn't viable due to pipe depth, collapse, or access constraints, we perform precise open-cut sewer replacement with minimal disruption. We backfill, compact, and restore disturbed areas as part of every job.

📍

Point Repair & Spot Patching

If the damage is isolated to one section of pipe, a targeted point repair avoids full-line replacement and saves thousands. Our camera inspection pinpoints exactly where the repair is needed so we don't do unnecessary work.

⚠️ Warning Signs

Signs Your Austin Home Has Sewer Line Problems

Austin's expansive clay soils, root-heavy tree population, and aging infrastructure make sewer problems more common than in most Texas cities. Know the warning signs before a backup floods your home.

🚿

Multiple Drains Backing Up

If your toilet, sink, and shower are all slow or backing up at the same time, the problem isn't individual clogs — it's your main sewer line. This requires immediate professional attention.

👃

Sewage Odors Indoors or in Yard

Persistent sewer gas smell inside your home or patches of unusually lush green grass in your yard signal a leaking or broken sewer line that is releasing waste underground.

🌊

Water Pooling in Yard

Wet, soggy patches in your yard — especially along the path from your house to the street — suggest a sewer line leak underground. Austin's clay soil absorbs sewage slowly, making these patches persist for days.

📈

Spike in Water or Sewer Bills

A sudden increase in your City of Austin utility bill without changes in usage habits can indicate a hidden sewer leak or damage that's allowing ground infiltration — wasting water and increasing treatment costs.
